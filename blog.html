Hunter x Hunter Is a Novel Disguised as a Manga

Have you ever read a manga where the sheer amount of text takes up two-thirds of the page? Yes — that is exactly what it feels like reading Hunter x Hunter. And on top of that, you basically need a degree in physics just to follow what’s going on. That’s when it hit me: this isn’t structured like a typical manga at all. It reads like a novel.

Most manga rely on visual momentum: big panels, impact shots, clean pacing, dialogue that moves quickly so the action can breathe. Hunter x Hunter does the opposite. It explains, then explains the explanation, then gives you internal monologue about the explanation.

Entire chapters can feel like reading dense prose with illustrations attached. The narration becomes heavy. Strategic thinking gets dissected line by line. Motivations are unpacked in detail instead of being implied through action.

In many series, fights are spectacles. Here, fights are essays. Characters don’t just attack. They calculate probabilities, analyze emotional states, impose restrictions on themselves, and construct layered conditions around abilities. The panels sometimes feel less like action scenes and more like pages from a psychological thriller.

That density changes the experience. You’re not just watching events unfold — you’re processing arguments. Every decision has reasoning behind it. Every ability has a philosophy behind it. Every conflict becomes a mental chess match before it becomes physical.

And that’s why it feels like a novel. Novels are allowed to slow down. Novels are allowed to linger inside a character’s head. Novels are allowed to trust that the reader will sit still and think.

Most manga optimize for momentum. Hunter x Hunter optimizes for depth. It doesn’t just show you what happens. It tells you why it happens, how it happens, and what it means while it’s happening.

At some point, you stop flipping pages for hype — and start turning them like you would with a dense book you don’t want to skim. That’s when you realize: it’s not a manga pretending to be complex. It’s a novel that just happens to be drawn.
